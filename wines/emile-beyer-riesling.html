üåç The Alsace Region

Alsace occupies a unique position in French wine, hugging the eastern slopes of the Vosges Mountains along the German border. The Vosges create a rain shadow that makes Alsace one of the driest and sunniest regions in France, while the cool continental climate preserves bright acidity. Unlike other French regions, Alsace wines are labeled by grape variety rather than terroir.
