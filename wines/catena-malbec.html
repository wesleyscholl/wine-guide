üåç Mendoza, Argentina

Mendoza is Argentina's premier wine region, nestled in the shadow of the Andes mountains. The high altitude (vineyards range from 3,000 to 5,000 feet) means intense UV exposure, which creates thick-skinned grapes with deep color and concentrated flavors. The dry desert climate and snowmelt irrigation from the Andes create ideal growing conditions for Malbec, which has found its spiritual home in this dramatic landscape.
