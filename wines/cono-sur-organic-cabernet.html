üåç Where It Comes From

The Colchagua Valley is one of Chile's premier wine regions, located about 100 miles south of Santiago. Protected by the Andes Mountains to the east and cooled by Pacific breezes from the west, this valley enjoys a Mediterranean climate perfect for Cabernet Sauvignon. The organic vineyards benefit from Chile's naturally isolated geography - the country's deserts, mountains, and ocean create natural barriers against pests and diseases.
