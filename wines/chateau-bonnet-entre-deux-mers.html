üåç The Entre-Deux-Mers Region

Entre-Deux-Mers, meaning "between two seas," refers to the land between the Garonne and Dordogne rivers in the heart of Bordeaux. This hilly region is Bordeaux's largest appellation for white wines, known for producing fresh, aromatic whites at excellent value. The clay and limestone soils, combined with the tempering influence of the rivers, create ideal conditions for the classic Bordeaux white grape trio.
