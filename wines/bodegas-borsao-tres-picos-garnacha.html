üåç Campo de Borja, Spain

Campo de Borja, located in the Arag√≥n region of northeastern Spain, has been called the "Empire of Garnacha" for good reason. The region's ancient vines, some planted over a century ago, grow on rocky, mineral-rich soils at elevations up to 2,600 feet. The continental climate with hot days and cool nights produces wines of remarkable concentration and freshness. Bodegas Borsao is a cooperative of over 2,400 growers who have pooled their resources to create world-class wines.
