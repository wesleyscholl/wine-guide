Giuseppe Mascarello e Figlio has been crafting legendary Barolo since 1881, making them one of Piedmont's most revered traditional producers. Known as the "King of Wines," Barolo represents the pinnacle of Italian winemaking. üåç The Barolo Region

Barolo is a small DOCG zone in the Langhe hills of Piedmont, northwestern Italy. The unique combination of altitude, climate, and calcareous marl soils creates conditions that bring out the best in Nebbiolo grapes. The region's steep hillsides and varied exposures produce wines of remarkable complexity, with each cru expressing distinct terroir characteristics. Barolo requires a minimum of 38 months aging before release.
