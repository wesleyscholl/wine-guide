üåç The Montalcino Region

Montalcino is a hilltop town in southern Tuscany, known for producing some of Italy's greatest wines. The region's unique terroir, with its combination of clay, limestone, and galestro soils at elevations up to 2,000 feet, creates ideal conditions for Sangiovese Grosso (locally called Brunello). The strict DOCG regulations require 100% Sangiovese and minimum aging of 4 years for Brunello, resulting in wines of remarkable depth, complexity, and longevity.
