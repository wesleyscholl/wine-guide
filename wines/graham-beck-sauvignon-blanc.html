üåç The Robertson Region

Robertson is a warm, dry wine region in South Africa's Western Cape, known for its limestone-rich soils along the Breede River Valley. Despite the warm climate, cool breezes from the Indian Ocean moderate temperatures, allowing white grapes like Sauvignon Blanc to retain their natural acidity and freshness. The region is also famous for its horse studs and the Robertson Wine Valley's scenic beauty.
