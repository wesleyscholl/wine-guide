üçá About This Wine

Amarone is one of Italy's most prestigious and unique wines, made using the ancient appassimento method. Grapes are harvested and dried for 3-4 months in special drying lofts, concentrating their sugars and flavors before fermentation. The result is a wine of extraordinary richness, depth, and complexity. Masi, founded in 1772, is one of Amarone's most respected producers, combining traditional techniques with modern expertise.

üåç Valpolicella, Veneto, Italy

Valpolicella, meaning "valley of many cellars," is located in the Veneto region of northeastern Italy, just north of Verona. The region's hillside vineyards, planted on volcanic and limestone soils, have been producing wine for over 2,000 years. The appassimento technique, unique to this area, transforms the local grape varieties into wines of exceptional concentration. Masi's vineyards span some of the most prestigious areas, including the historic Classico zone.
