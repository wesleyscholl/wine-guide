üåç The Beaujolais Region

Beaujolais lies just south of Burgundy in eastern France, famous for its granite-rich soils and the Gamay grape. The region produces some of the world's most approachable red wines, known for their bright fruit, low tannins, and easy-drinking nature. Georges Duboeuf helped put Beaujolais on the world map, especially through the famous Beaujolais Nouveau celebrations each November.
