üåç The Douro Valley

The Douro Valley, Portugal's oldest demarcated wine region (since 1756), is a UNESCO World Heritage site known for its dramatic terraced vineyards cascading down to the Douro River. The region's schist soils and continental climate create wines with intense concentration and aging potential. Quinta do Crasto's 170 hectares of vineyards are planted on steep terraces that require heroic viticulture.
